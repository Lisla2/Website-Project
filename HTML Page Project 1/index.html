American Monster Folklore

The United States has a rich tradition of tales surrounding strange and unverified creatures. Indigenous people and later settlers held beliefs over the existence of powerful and sometimes even malicious entities. Each region of the country has its own unique monster story.

Western US

Western Native Americans long told stories reminiscent of the legend of Bigfoot.

Southwestern US

Tribes in the region such as the Navajo believe in the Skinwalker , a shape-shifting creature of malicious and dangerous intent.

Southern US

The Thunderbird is a powerful being in local Native culture. It has the ability to control thunder and is either an omen, protector or good luck sign to whomever it appears.

Northeastern US

Similar to the Southwestern Skinwalker, the Wendigo is another shape-shifting evil spirit determined to bring misery to humans that encounter them.
